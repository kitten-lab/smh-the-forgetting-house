# Something Mattered Here
#### THE FORGETTING HOUSE

v.0.1


# Game Intention Overview

You cannot remember who or where you are.  
Memory is non-existent, and focus is limited and fragile.

**Something Mattered Here: The Forgetting House** is a non-linear, narrative-driven card puzzle game about reconstructing identity through fragmented memories. It asks the question:

_Even when all meaning is missing, can we remember that something mattered here_?

Players explore a procedurally reconfigured house where each room contains a puzzle called a Matter. By combining Emotion and Skill cards, players uncover narrative fragments that accumulate in the Memory Deck. Fragments combine into Memories and eventually Stories, allowing players to reconstruct an interpretation of their past.

Each run (fugue) represents a cycle of forgetting and rediscovery, with emotional states influencing the player's actions and perception of the house.

## Design Intentions

**Meaning Through Interpretation-** Players do not uncover a single objective truth. Instead, they construct their own interpretation of events through the Memories and Stories they create.

**Emotion as Mechanics-** Emotional states influence gameplay directly, shaping what actions can be performed, and how easy or challenging it is to complete them.

**The House as Self-** the Forgetting House is not just a location but a metaphor for the protagonist’s fragmented identity.

# Gameplay Mechanics Overview

**The game begins in an amnesia state.**

_The Forgetting House is filled with rooms, and something mattered in each of them. Players must combine Emotions and Skills to solve each room’s Matter, unlocking Fragments of memories and additional Skills along the way._

## Focus, Forgetting, and the Fugue

The Forgetting House is a game of recursion and cyclical forgetting. Gameplay centers around using action points (Focus Points) on the puzzles (Matters) found in each Room of the House.   

All Actions taken by players in the Forgetting House cost Focus Points. The number of Focus Points a player has per run (Fugue) determines how many attempts they have to solve the Matters before they _forget_ again.

Each Fugue consists of the Actions taken with the number of Focus Points available in that round. Focus Points may be used on:

- Moving from room to room
- Combining playable cards to solve a Matter in the House
- Combining cards in the Memory Deck

All Actions use Focus Points, regardless of the Action’s success.  The number of Focus Points an Action requires is modified by the playable cards used and Baseline emotional state mood of each Fugue.

Each successfully completed Action may return:

- A Focus Point bonus
- Narrative fragments (Memory Deck cards)
- bonus playable cards

Each unsuccessfully completed Action may return:

- bonus playable cards

When players run out of Action Points, they _forget_. Forgetting results in the loss or decay of the playable cards the player has in their hand.

On each new Fugue, players “awaken” again in the threshold location of the house.

This location acts as the round-reset recursion point: players will awaken here each time they _forget_.

## Starting with the Baseline: Round Modifiers

At the start of the Fugue, players will be assigned a Baseline Emotional State. This Baseline state affects the number of Focus Points for the round, as well as any Emotion cards which will be dealt for the run.

The Baseline Emotional State alters the default conditions of each Fugue and the cost of each Action in Focus Points. Baseline States may modify the following conditions:

- **Focus Points-** A +/- modifier on the number of Focus Points
- **Emotion Cards-** Increases/decreases the odds of some Emotion Cards
- **Guaranteed Emotion-** Awards an Emotion Card of its same name
- **Skill Suppressors-** May prevent certain Skill Cards use during that round

_Sometimes the only way to proceed is to have the right Baseline card in a Fugue._

## Action Playable Cards: Emotions and Skills

Players have an inventory of playable cards. There are 2 different card decks used for Actions within the game: Emotion Cards and Skill Cards.

### Emotion Cards

At the start of each Fugue, players are assigned a Baseline Emotional State and dealt a hand of Emotion Cards. The player will be given a number of Emotion Cards with a range of decay rates.

**Decay Rates:**

- **Temporary Single Use-** 
  The card will be used and removed from the hand when used on a puzzle, whether or not the solution succeeds or fails.
- **Temporary Single Fugue-** 
  The card will remain in the hand, regardless of use, until the focus = 0 and the next fugue round begins.
- **Temporary Multi-Fugue-** 
  The card will remain in the hand for _n_ number of Fugues. The total remaining rounds will be displayed on the card.
- **Permanent**- 
  The card never decays (only awarded for solving Matters or making Stories)

**Temporary Emotions:** These cards are awarded at the beginning of each Fugue and from certain actions in game. These cards will burn off according to their decay rate assigned when dealt at the beginning of the Fugue.

**Permanent Emotions:** These cards are awarded as you solve Matters or create Stories in the Memory Deck. These cards are permanent and will remain in your inventory after every fugue.

Emotion Cards contain different feelings. Depending on the Matter being solved, different Feeling states will result in different solutions available, and the resulting Fragment Cards the player may obtain.

### Skill Cards

In addition to Emotion Cards, the hand also contains Skill Cards. Players begin the game with one randomly assigned Skill Card and one base Skill Card required to solve the first puzzle. To acquire additional Skill Cards, the player must solve puzzles in the house.

Skills never decay but may be impacted by Baseline states.

Skills include cards such as:

- Reading (“Reading Glasses”)
- Writing (“My Favorite Pen”)
- Contemplation (“The Long Stare”)
- Aggression (“Firestarter”)
- Complaining (“Lamentations”)
- Dissociation (“A Blind Eye”)

### Baseline Emotional Alignment

Actions taken using Emotion and Skill Cards that align with Baseline require less Focus, while actions taken using conflicting Emotion and Skill Cards require additional Focus.

Focus Cost Modifiers:

- **Aligned Card:** Reduced Focus cost
- **Neutral Card:** Normal Focus cost
- **Opposed Card:** Increased Focus cost

This system reflects the mental effort required to act against one's emotional state.

## Exploring the Forgetting House

The Forgetting House is a procedurally generated map of 10-20 pre-defined Rooms, each containing some Matter to solve and discoverable Fragment Cards for use in the Memory Deck. Each round, the map of the House is reconfigured, creating a unique layout of the rooms for each round.

The player always begins in the Threshold Room, a bedroom.  

From the Threshold, a hallway leads to other Rooms in the house. This hallway is procedurally generated as well and may contain as few as 1 doorway or as many as 5.

Each Room in the House contains at least two Doors: one entered through and at least 1 exit to another room. Doors are locked until the Matter is solved within the Room.

The number of Doors in a Room is equal to the number of Matters that can be solved in the Room. Once a Matter is solved, its associated Doorway remains permanently unlocked.

Even when the house layout changes during future fugues, the Doorway in that Room will remain accessible.

Rooms containing multiple Matters may be solved in any order.

Each solved Matter unlocks one additional Doorway from the Room.

Each Room is a named location that can be found in the House. For example, the player may encounter:

- THE KITCHEN
- THE GUEST BEDROOM
- THE PARLOR
- THE GARDEN

Or more symbolic Rooms like:

- THE EMPTY BALCONY
- THE ALCHEMY LAB
- THE SUNKEN LIBRARY

Each room contains:

- 1 or more Matter puzzle(s)
- Fragment Cards (narrative fragments for use in the Memory Deck)
- Bonus playable Card rewards (associated with the Room)
- Additional Doorways through the House

Moving between Rooms costs 1 Focus Point.

This represents the mental effort required to continue searching the house for meaning.

### What’s the Matter? Acting On Things

Each Room in the Forgetting House contains a puzzle known as a Matter. A Matter can be solved with the right combination of playable cards. Multiple solutions may exist for different Matters, resulting in different Fragment Cards for use in the Memory Deck.

**Example Matter:** An old letter.

**Solutions:**

- Curiosity + Reading Glasses = Fragment Card of the letters contents.
- Anger + Firestarter =  Fragment Card related to loss.

Incorrect combinations result in the loss of Focus Points and generate temporary Fragment Cards.

**For example, the following would not work:**

- Curiosity + Firestarter = loss of Focus Points, a temporary Fragment Card (I hate when things are destroyed.)

- Apathy + Reading Glasses = loss of Focus Points, a temporary Fragment Card (I just don’t care right now.)

Each attempt to solve a Matter costs some amount of Focus Points. The number of Focus Points required will change depending on your Baseline mood and the combination of cards played.

Successfully solving a Matter will reward the player with a Focus Point bonus, new Fragment Cards, and additional Skill cards.

## Making Meaning: The Memory Deck

The Memory Deck is a secondary screen interface apart from the House itself. During gameplay, the player will collect Fragment and Memory Cards which may be used in the Memory Deck.

Meaning is the primary narrative reward of the Forgetting House, and the Memory Deck is where meaning is made.

Within the Memory Deck, players can review and combine collected Fragment Cards.

By combining compatible cards, players construct higher-level Memory Cards that reconstruct the meaning of the House.

As the Memory Deck grows, players may begin forming complete Memory Structures called Stories.

These structures represent interpretations of what happened in the Forgetting House and ultimately shape the player's final understanding of the narrative.

### Fragment Cards

Fragment cards are awarded at different moments during gameplay. You may earn Fragment cards:

- At Fugue start (random occurrence)
- Upon finding a new Room
- At failed solution of a Matter
- At successful completion of a Matter
- By combining other Fragments

These Fragments are stored in the Memory Deck. Some Fragment cards represent temporary thoughts, while others represent events or relationships.

Fragment Cards may include:

- **Identity Fragments** – short reflections or emotional responses
- **Event Fragments** – pieces of what happened in the house
- **Relationship Fragments**– information about the people who lived here
- **Loss Fragments** – moments connected to grief or disappearance

Over multiple fugues, the player collects Fragments which can be combined to reconstruct the meaning of the House.

### Memory Cards

Memory cards are awarded by combining Fragment cards in the Memory Deck. You may also earn Memories rarely:

- At Fugue start
- By successfully completing a Matter

Memory cards are also stored in the Memory Deck. Like Fragments, Memories may represent:

- Events
- Relationships
- Losses
- Goals
- Dreams

By combining Fragment Cards, players create Memory Cards.

Memory Cards can then be combined to form Story Cards.

### Story Cards

Story Cards are awarded only by combining Memory Cards with Emotion Cards. Different combinations of Memory Cards and Emotion Cards will result in different Stories. Stories Cards represent one of the following Story Pillars:

- Who you were
- Who mattered to you
- What you were trying to do
- What you lost
- Your Dream (what remains)

It is possible to generate more than one Story Card related to any of the Story Pillars. For example, the player may have no _What Was Lost_ Story Cards but have 3 or 4 _Who I Was_ Story Cards.

Story Card examples:

- WHAT WAS LOST: BOOMING BUSINESS  
“I had built the whole place myself. Every inch. I remember how much it mattered to me. How did it all fall apart?”  

- WHAT WAS LOST: THE LOVE I ALWAYS DREAMED OF  
“They were so beautiful. Why did I never notice? Why couldn’t I have seen what was right in front of me?”  

- WHO WAS I: THE DELICATE PAINTER  
“These hands weren’t made for hard work. I remember spreading the paint on every canvas until I was blinded by the colors.”  

- WHO WAS I: THE SYSTEM ARCHITECT  
“I could have built it. If only I tried. I swear I could have built anything.”

Story Cards alter the state of the House. When a Story Card is created, one or more of the following effects may occur:

- New Rooms are added to the map configuration
- New Doorways are added to Rooms
- New flavors of Fragment Cards may appear
- Permanent Emotion Cards may be granted
- Skill Cards may be granted

In order to “complete the game”, the player must have at least one of each class of Story card.

### Managing the Story

Once players have created Story Cards, they may attempt to reconstruct what happened. There is a slot for each of the Story Pillars in the Memory Deck. The Story Cards placed in the Story Pillar spaces act as modifiers to the Fugue, offering the player unique Emotion and Skill cards only found with Story Cards placed.

### Reconstructing What Happened: The End Game

As the player progresses, more of the Story Pillar positions will become filled. Placing one of each Story Card on the Story Pillars will “complete the game”.

The resulting combination determines the narrative reconstruction of the player's past. Different Story combinations will produce different interpretations of the events that led to the fugue state. The results of the combination will reveal a story for the player of what happened that led to the fugue state awakening.

This is considered the end game state but does not end the game. The player may continue to construct meaning with new cards, changing the Story Cards in the Story Pillars to find new meaning while they continue to explore the House and its remaining Rooms.
